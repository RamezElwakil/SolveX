8-Week Program Roadmap

A modern, hands-on journey from curiosity to real-world research. Each week builds your skills and confidence step by step.

1 Foundations of Scientific Research
Students are introduced to the core principles of scientific research. This week focuses on understanding what research is, why it matters, and how it shapes the world around us. Learners will explore real examples of impactful student-led projects and begin thinking critically about problems they care about.
Key Outcomes: Understand the purpose of research and identify a topic of personal or community relevance.
View Week
2 From Curiosity to a Research Question
This week guides students through the process of refining a general interest into a specific, actionable research question. They'll learn how to spot meaningful problems, write clear problem statements, and explore simple frameworks for problem-solving.
Key Outcomes: Formulate a clear and testable research question.
View Week
3 Investigating What’s Been Done
Students will dive into existing knowledge about their topic, learning how to conduct a basic literature review. They’ll explore how other researchers, students, and professionals have approached similar problems, and use that knowledge to shape their own approach.
Key Outcomes: Build context for the project by summarizing prior research and identifying gaps.
View Week
4 Designing the Investigation
In this week, students learn how to plan their experiment or investigation. They'll understand types of variables, how to make fair comparisons, and how to collect meaningful data. They'll also create a basic experimental or data collection plan.
Key Outcomes: Create a step-by-step plan for gathering and analyzing data.
View Week
5 Building and Testing the Solution
This hands-on week focuses on prototyping and beginning the data collection process. Students are introduced to tools and platforms like Arduino (if relevant) and practice running small-scale tests of their solution. They’ll analyze early data and adjust their plan if needed.
Key Outcomes: Build a working prototype or initial solution and collect preliminary results.
View Week
6 Presenting and Evaluating
Students will learn how to present their research in a professional and understandable way. They’ll create posters or digital presentations and learn how to evaluate their findings using test plans. This helps them practice communicating complex ideas simply.
Key Outcomes: Create a visual and verbal explanation of the project and test outcomes.
View Week
7 Impact and Innovation
Now students connect their research to real-world challenges. They'll explore how their work can align with global goals like the UN SDGs, and how to structure their idea into a basic innovation model using tools like the Business Model Canvas.
Key Outcomes: Define the social or practical value of the project and map out its application.
View Week
8 Presentation & Next Steps
Wrap up your project, reflect on your journey, and learn how to share your work with a wider audience. Get tips for competitions, publishing, and future research opportunities.
Key Outcomes: Confidently present your research and plan your next steps as a young scientist.
