❓ WEEK 2: From Curiosity to a Research Question

Purpose:

Teach students how to transform general interests into specific, focused, and researchable questions.

📘 Explanation:

❓ A good research question is the engine of your investigation. This week focuses on helping students take a broad curiosity and focus it into a specific, testable question using structured thinking.

We use the funnel method:

  • Broad topic → Pollution
  • Focused subtopic → Air pollution near schools
  • Specific question → "What is the PM2.5 air pollution level at school gates during rush hour?"

🧩 Example: Problem Tree

Main Problem: Low student concentration in class

Causes (Roots):

  • High noise levels at home
  • Lack of sleep
  • Poor diet

Effects (Branches):

  • Poor grades
  • Behavioral issues
  • Low motivation

By identifying a root cause (e.g., lack of sleep), students can form a research question like:

"How does sleep duration affect concentration levels in high school students?"

🎯 Objective:

Transform an interest or issue into a well-crafted research question.

✅ How to Achieve It:

  • Brainstorm issues of personal interest
  • Build a problem tree
  • Draft three possible research questions
  • Use the FINER criteria to refine your question

💬 Discussion Questions:

  • Why is it important to focus your question?
  • What makes a question "investigable"?

Quiz: Check Your Understanding

1. Which of the following is the best research question?
2. A research question should be:
3. What type of question should you avoid?
